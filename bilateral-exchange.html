Bilateral Exchange Program

Expand your academic horizons with international exchange opportunities at Ala-Too International University

What is bilateral programs?

At Ala-Too International University (AIU), we are proud to collaborate with over 100 universities worldwide through our bilateral programs. These initiatives create valuable opportunities for student and faculty exchanges, joint research projects, and dual degree programs. By partnering with institutions in countries such as France, Germany, Japan, China, Malaysia, Hungary, the UK, Poland, South Korea, the USA, and many others, we offer our community a truly global academic experience.

Our bilateral programs allow students to study abroad, gaining international experience and expanding their academic horizons. Faculty members can engage in teaching and research exchanges, sharing knowledge and expertise across borders. Additionally, participants in these programs benefit from enhanced cultural understanding and a global network of contacts.

Join AIU and explore the world through our extensive network of bilateral programs!

Eligibility Criteria

Study Period

Cannot be enrolled in first or last semester of studies

English Proficiency

Minimum B2 level required

Academic Performance

Minimum GPA score of 70

Required Documents

Official Transcript from Office of Student Affairs
Copy of International Passport
Certificate of English Language Skills
Bilateral Student Mobility Programme Application Form

Application Process

1

Briefing Seminar

Applicants can participate in Briefing Seminar to get detailed information about grant based and self-financed student mobility programmes and ask questions from officials.

2

Document Submission

Applicants have to submit required documents before the deadline. Spring semester — November 30th, Fall semester – May 15th.

3

University Selection

While completing application form applicants have to select three universities from list of partner universities. Applicants have to evaluate availability of the stream and department through partner universities' websites.

4

Allocation Process

The allocation process by Office of International Relations is based on GPA score of applicants.

5

Additional Documents

Depending on host university's requirements the allocated applicants have to submit additional documents and to fill up host university's Application Form and Learning Agreement.

Visa & Accommodation

Visa Information

EU residents have visa free privileges (up to 60 days) to Kyrgyz Republic. The International Relations Office will send an Invitation Letter in case you need a visa to enter Kyrgyz Republic.

After the expiration of visa free days, AIU IRO is responsible for visa extension till the end of your study period.

Accommodation Options

Dormitories and communal facilities are provided for students. Accommodation requests will only be considered whether the accommodation request is submitted at least 2 months before the expected date of arrival.

Students may also choose other private housing best suited to their lifestyle and location on their own.
